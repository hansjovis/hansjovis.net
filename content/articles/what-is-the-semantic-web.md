## What is the semantic web?

The <dfn>semantic web</dfn> is an extension of the world wide web where **data** is interlinked rather than **documents**.

## What are the advantages of the semantic web?

There are two main advantages of the semantic web.

The first advantage is that the data is structured and machine-readable. This means that computers can read and understand the data without much hassle, under the assumption that they know **how** the data is structured.

This in contrast with the generally unstructured nature of webpages right now. With 'unstructured' I mean that the information on webpages is for a large part encoded within text, images, video and sound. Although Artificial Intelligence has come a long way in understanding this kind of data, it is still difficult for computers to do.

The second advantage is that this structured data can be interlinked. This gives computers the ability to connect data between webpages and even infer new connections.

## What can we do with the semantic web?

It is this last advantage, the fact that the semantic web gives computers the ability to make connections between structured data **on different webpages**, that gives rise to pretty powerful web applications.

In fact, we already saw the value of these kinds of applications in the last decade in three forms: social networks, knowledge graphs and content platforms.

However, the social networks, knowledge graphs and content platforms of the last decade have one weak point: they are closed off.

For example, the data on Facebook is only available to the people that have registered and to Facebook itself.

Perhaps more importantly: these kinds of networks fence off this data into their own private semantic webs, making it almost impossible to relate these different networks to each other.

## What are the barriers for implementing the semantic web?

The main barrier for implementing the semantic web is getting the vast amount of data on the web actually structured and linked.

Linked data is only as valuable as the structured data there already is and can be linked to. Why would people bother adding linked data to their website, when other people wouldn't do the same? This kind of network effect means that the semantic web would only gather steam when there is a substantial amount of structured data already.
